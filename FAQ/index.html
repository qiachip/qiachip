    KR2202

    Question 01. Why can my remote control Relay 1 but not Relay 2?

    Answer:

    Your setup is wrong, so your remote can only control Relay 1. The receiver is not broken.

    You need to pair it again with the steps below.

    1. Press the Learning button 8 times to reset.
    2. Press the Learning button 1 time for Momentary mode.
    3. Press the button for Relay 1. Wait until the light flashes, then turns off.
    4. Press the button for Relay 2. Wait until the light flashes, then turns on.

    This sets both relays.

    Question 02. Why do both button A and button B control Relay 1 only after Momentary pairing?

    Answer:

    Your pairing steps are wrong, so button A and button B both control Relay 1 only. The receiver is not broken.

    You need to pair it again with the steps below.

    1. Press the Learning button 8 times to reset.
    2. Press the Learning button 1 time for Momentary mode.
    3. Press button A. Wait until the light flashes, then turns off.
    4. Press button B. Wait until the light flashes, then turns on.

    This sets both relays.

    Question 03. Momentary, Toggle, and Latching mode. What is the difference?

    Answer:

    KR2202 has three working modes.

    • Momentary mode: hold the button, the relay turns on. Release the button, the relay turns off.
    • Toggle mode: press the button once, the relay turns on. Press the same button again, the relay turns off.
    • Latching mode: press button A, Relay 1 turns on. Press button B, Relay 2 turns on and Relay 1 turns off. Press the STOP button, the active relay turns off.

    Use Momentary mode for hold-to-run control. Use Toggle mode for on / off control. Use Latching mode when two directions must not turn on at the same time.

    Question 04. Do the two relays trigger at the same time or one by one?

    Answer:

    No. KR2202 does not have an auto-sequence mode.

    The two relays are controlled by different remote buttons. In Momentary mode or Toggle mode, button A controls Relay 1 and button B controls Relay 2.

    Question 05. How do I set two remotes to control Relay 1 and Relay 2 on KR2202?

    Answer:

    Pair one button on the first remote to Relay 1. Pair one button on the second remote to Relay 2. Do both in the same setup.

    Example for Toggle mode:

    1. Press the Learning button on the receiver 2 times. Wait until the light turns off.
    2. Press the button on the first remote for Relay 1. Wait until the light flashes, then turns off.
    3. Press the button on the second remote for Relay 2. Wait until the light flashes, then turns on.

    If the pairing is wrong, press the Learning button 8 times to reset. Reset will delete all paired remotes.

    Question 06. Can I pair multiple remotes to channels A and B in Momentary mode?

    Answer:

    Yes. KR2202 can pair multiple remotes to channels A and B in Momentary mode.

    For each new remote, pair both buttons in the same Momentary setup. Do not pair button A alone.

    Press the Learning button 8 times only if you want to reset. Reset will delete all paired remotes.

    Question 07. Why does my 3-button UP / STOP / DOWN remote not work for motor control in Toggle mode?

    Answer:

    Toggle mode is not the right mode for UP / STOP / DOWN motor control. Use Latching mode instead.

    Pair UP and DOWN in the same Latching mode setup:

    1. Press the Learning button 8 times to reset.
    2. Press the Learning button 3 times to enter Latching mode.
    3. Press UP. Wait until the light flashes, then turns off.
    4. Press DOWN. Wait until the light flashes, then turns on.

    After this setup: - Press UP, the motor runs up. - Press DOWN, the motor runs down and UP turns off. - Press STOP, the active relay turns off.

    For motor control, the up and down directions must not turn on at the same time.

    Question 08. How do I use KR2202 Momentary mode to control a two-direction motor with button A and button B?

    Answer:

    Yes. You can use KR2202 Momentary mode for a two-direction motor.

    1. Press the Learning button 1 time. Wait until the light turns off.
    2. Press button A on the remote. Wait until the light flashes, then turns off.
    3. Press button B on the remote. Wait until the light flashes, then turns on.

    In Momentary mode, the relay turns on while you hold the button. For a two-direction motor, both directions must not turn on at the same time.

    If the motor system has its own control input, connect KR2202 to that input. Do not connect KR2202 directly to the motor power wires.

    Question 09. Does the KR2202 relay stay on after power loss?

    Answer:

    No. The relay cannot stay on when the power is off.

    KR2202 can remember the paired remote and working mode after power loss. But the relay needs power to stay on. After power comes back, the next relay action depends on the mode and remote command.

    Question 10. Is the KR2202 receiver powered by 220V, or are the relay contacts rated for 220V?

    Answer:

    Yes, the KR2202 receiver can be powered by 220V AC. But the relay contacts do not output 220V by themselves.

    The relay output is a dry contact. This means it works like a switch. It does not give power by itself. The terminals are NO, COM, and NC.

    Question 11. Is KR2202 compatible with 230V AC?

    Answer:

    Yes. KR2202 can work with 230V AC power.

    Its input voltage range is AC 85V-250V, so 230V AC is within the supported range.

    Question 12. How should I wire KR2202 for my load?

    Answer:

    It depends on what load you want to control.

    Use the wiring diagram that matches your load, such as a motor, lamp, or other device. Turn off power before wiring. Do not reverse L and N. Keep the load within Max 1100W.

    L and N power the KR2202 receiver itself. The relay terminals do not output power by themselves. They are dry contacts. They work like switches for the load circuit you connect.

    Question 13. Can one KR2202 remote control two garage door motors?

    Answer:

    It depends on what you mean by garage door motor.

    If you mean a garage door opener with a control input, yes. KR2202 can trigger the control input. It works like pressing a wall button.

    Do not use KR2202 to drive bare garage door motors directly. KR2202 is a relay receiver, not a motor controller.

    Question 14. Can KR2202 control a winch raise and lower motor?

    Answer:

    Yes, but only if the winch motor matches KR2202.

    Check the motor voltage, running current, and starting current first. KR2202 input is AC 85V-250V. The rated load is Max 1100W. For raise and lower control, the up and down directions must not turn on at the same time.

    Question 15. Is KR2202 suitable for a roller shutter or garage shutter motor?

    Answer:

    Yes. KR2202 can work for some roller shutter or garage shutter motors.

    Key points:

    • The shutter motor or controller must match KR2202 power and load limits: AC 85V-250V input and Max 1100W load.
    • Momentary mode is a good choice. The motor runs while you hold the button.
    • If the shutter has a manual wall switch, connect KR2202 COM and NO across the switch terminals. This adds remote control.
    Question 16. Can KR2202 use button A and button B for gate open and close in Momentary mode?

    Answer:

    Yes, if your gate controller has dry contact OPEN and CLOSE inputs.

    Use Relay 1 for OPEN and Relay 2 for CLOSE. Set KR2202 to Momentary mode. The gate will respond while you hold the button. Connect KR2202 only to dry contact input terminals.

    Question 17. How do I identify a 3-wire roller shutter motor? Does KR2202 stop at the end position?

    Answer:

    No. Do not assume the third wire is ground. KR2202 does not stop the shutter at the end position by itself.

    A 3-wire shutter motor may be Live / Neutral / Ground, or it may be Common / Up / Down. Check the motor label or manual before wiring. KR2202 turns the relay on and off. The shutter motor or controller must have its own limit switch.

    Question 18. Why does KR2202 work with no load but fail when I connect a pool cover motor?

    Answer:

    KR2202 is probably not the broken part. The problem is the motor load or the motor control method.

    The two most likely reasons are:

    1. The motor starting current is too high. A motor needs more current when it starts than when it runs. Even if the running power is under Max 1100W, the starting current may be too high for KR2202. Do not use KR2202 to switch the motor power directly. Use a motor controller or contactor. Or check the starting current before wiring.

    2. The pool cover motor may need its own control input. KR2202 is a relay receiver, not a motor controller. It works like a switch through NO and COM. If the pool cover system has a wall switch or control input, connect KR2202 to that input. Do not switch the motor power wires directly.

    Check the motor label or manual for starting current and control input type. If you are not sure, ask an electrician before wiring.

    Question 19. Can one remote control two KR2202 receivers in Momentary mode and Toggle mode?

    Answer:

    Yes, it is possible, but we do not recommend it if the two receivers are close to each other.

    One remote sends the same RF signal. If both receivers are in range, both will receive the signal and operate. Keep the two receivers far apart so they are not in the same range. Or use separate remotes.

    Question 20. Can I add a signal amplifier to KR2202 to reach 100 meters?

    Answer:

    No. We do not recommend adding a signal amplifier to KR2202.

    KR2202 uses 433.92MHz remote control. To improve distance, fully extend the receiver antenna. Keep it away from metal and motors. If you need about 100 meters, use a longer-range remote with an antenna. Do not change the receiver.

    KR1201A

    Question 01. Does KR1201A support momentary mode?

    Answer:

    Yes. KR1201A supports momentary mode. Hold the remote button, the relay turns on. Release the button, the relay turns off.

    KR1201A also supports self-locking mode and interlocking mode. Choose the mode for your use.

    Question 02. In momentary mode, what happens if the remote moves out of range while I hold the button?

    Answer:

    The relay will turn off. Moving out of range is the same as releasing the button. The receiver no longer gets the signal.

    Question 03. Which mode should I use to reduce battery consumption?

    Answer:

    The receiver uses a small amount of power in standby. Momentary mode can reduce the active working time. This may help extend battery life.

    For longer operating time, use a large-capacity battery or a stable 12V DC power supply.

    Question 04. Will KR1201A lose the paired remotes or working mode after power loss?

    Answer:

    No. The relay cannot stay on when the power is off. KR1201A can remember the paired remotes and working mode after power loss.

    After power comes back, the receiver will work with the saved remotes and mode. Reset will delete the saved data.

    Question 05. Does toggle mode need to be set again after power loss?

    Answer:

    No. KR1201A will remember toggle mode after power loss. You do not need to pair or set it again after power is restored.

    Question 06. Can one KR1201A receiver work with more than one remote?

    Answer:

    Yes. One KR1201A receiver can work with multiple remotes. Pair the first remote, then pair each new remote the same way.

    Each paired remote can control the same receiver.

    Question 07. Can two remotes use the same working mode, such as delay mode or toggle mode?

    Answer:

    Yes. Multiple remotes can pair with the same receiver and use the same working mode.

    Question 08. Can KR1201A reverse a DC motor?

    Answer:

    No. KR1201A is a single-channel relay receiver. It can do simple on / off control. It cannot change the motor direction.

    To reverse a DC motor, you need to swap the positive and negative wires. This needs a two-channel relay or a motor controller. KR1201A cannot do this.

    Question 09. Can button B reverse a motor?

    Answer:

    No. KR1201A is a single-channel relay receiver. It cannot do forward and reverse motor control.

    For motor forward and reverse control, use a two-channel controller.

    Question 10. Can KR1201A control a servo motor?

    Answer:

    No. KR1201A is not made for servo motor control. Servo motors need a signal-based servo controller.

    If you need motor forward and reverse control, use a motor controller.

    Question 11. Can two KR1201A receivers control a DC motor left and right?

    Answer:

    No. KR1201A is not the right choice for DC motor direction control. Use a two-channel receiver or a motor controller.

    Question 12. Can KR1201A be powered by AC power?

    Answer:

    No. KR1201A is a DC receiver. Do not connect it to AC power.

    Use a DC power supply for KR1201A. If you need AC 110V or AC 220V control, choose a receiver made for that AC voltage.

    Question 13. Can KR1201A control AC 220V home appliances?

    Answer:

    No. KR1201A is a 12V DC receiver. Do not power it with AC 220V.

    If you need to control AC 220V appliances, use a relay module made for AC 220V.

    Question 14. Can KR1201A and the load use the same power supply?

    Answer:

    Yes, but only if the voltage and current match both the receiver and the load.

    Check that:

    • The receiver input voltage matches the power supply.
    • The load voltage matches the same power supply.
    • The power supply can provide enough current for both.

    Wrong voltage or not enough current can damage the device.

    Question 15. Can a 5V version work with a 3.7V Li-ion / 18650 battery?

    Answer:

    No. A 3.7V battery is lower than 5V. It may cause unstable work, shorter range, or the receiver may not work at all.

    Use a proper 5V power source for a 5V receiver.

    Question 16. What battery should I use for KR1201A?

    Answer:

    Use a 12V power source for KR1201A. A 12V lithium battery is better than AA batteries.

    AA batteries may work, but they have lower capacity and may run out fast. If the device stops working, check the battery first.

    Question 17. Can a 9V battery power KR1201A?

    Answer:

    No. KR1201A needs 12V DC. A 9V battery may light the indicator, but it cannot power the relay.

    Use a proper 12V power source.

    Question 18. Can two devices be connected using NO and NC?

    Answer:

    Yes. NO is normally open. NC is normally closed.

    Connect one load through NO and another load through NC. When the relay changes, one load turns on and the other turns off. Avoid short circuits.

    Question 19. Can the negative line be connected to COM?

    Answer:

    Many wiring diagrams connect the positive line to COM. The negative line can also connect to COM in some DC circuits, but only if you understand relay wiring.

    The key rule: the circuit must not create a short circuit.

    Question 20. Can KR1201A control two LED lights so that one is on while the other is off?

    Answer:

    Yes. Connect one LED through NO and the other LED through NC. When the relay changes, one LED turns on and the other turns off.

    For more complex control, use a multi-channel relay receiver.

    Question 21. What is the maximum remote-control distance?

    Answer:

    The actual distance depends on the remote model, antenna, environment, and interference.

    Some remotes claim 2 miles or 3 km. Real-world distance is much lower, around 1 km or less.

    Question 22. Can I connect an external antenna to KR1201A?

    Answer:

    Yes, but the new antenna must match the module specs. KR1201A has an antenna on the module. You can replace it for testing.

    A wrong antenna may not improve the distance. The module may also work poorly.

    Question 23. How do I reset KR1201A?

    Answer:

    Press the learning button 8 times to reset.

    After reset, all paired remotes and mode settings will be deleted. Pair the remotes and set the mode again.

    Question 24. Does KR1201A have memory storage, such as EEPROM?

    Answer:

    Yes. KR1201A has memory for paired remotes and mode settings.

    The module can store up to 20 remotes.

    Question 25. Can KR1201A be used for access control or automatic residential gates?

    Answer:

    Yes, for some access-control uses. KR1201A can provide dry contact control.

    For an automatic residential gate, it depends on the gate controller input and voltage. For AC 220V systems or high-power uses, choose a receiver made for that system.
